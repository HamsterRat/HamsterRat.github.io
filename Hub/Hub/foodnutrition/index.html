Nourish Up

Nourish Up addresses food insecurity in Mecklenburg County through food pantries, grocery home delivery, and prepared meal programs. Formed from the merger of two long-standing nonprofits, it supports thousands of neighbors each year with access to nutritious food.

Hope Street Food Pantry

Hope Street Food Pantry provides barrier-free access to nutritious food through weekly distributions and mobile pantry services. The organization focuses on dignity, accessibility, and community support for individuals and families facing hunger.

Visit Hope Street Food Pantry →

Feeding Charlotte

Feeding Charlotte works to reduce food waste and hunger by rescuing surplus food from local businesses and redistributing it to people in need. The organization emphasizes sustainability, community partnerships, and volunteer engagement.

Visit Feeding Charlotte →

Second Harvest Food Bank of Metrolina

Second Harvest Food Bank of Metrolina serves communities across North and South Carolina by distributing food through a large network of partner agencies. It also provides education, advocacy, and resources to address hunger at a regional level.

Visit Second Harvest Metrolina →

Meals on Wheels North Carolina

Meals on Wheels North Carolina supports local senior nutrition programs through advocacy, training, and statewide collaboration. The organization works to strengthen services that help older adults maintain health, independence, and quality of life.

What to Donate to a Food Bank

If you’re planning to donate food, choosing the right items helps food banks provide safe, nutritious meals to families in need. Food banks primarily accept nonperishable, shelf-stable items that can be safely stored and distributed.

Foods You Can Donate

  • Peanut butter
  • Canned soup, stew, and chili
  • Canned fruits and vegetables
  • Canned beans and canned fish
  • Pasta (whole-grain preferred)
  • Rice (brown rice preferred)
  • Cooking oils, dressings, and marinades that do not require refrigeration

Foods You Should Not Donate

  • Perishable items requiring refrigeration or freezing
  • Expired food or items past their sell-by or use-by dates
  • Leftovers or homemade food prepared in personal kitchens
  • Damaged, opened, dented, or bloated packaging
  • Baked goods made at home
  • Food packaged in glass containers
